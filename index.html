As president of the Hope for Young Leaders club, I have had a lot of experience with communication and organization. We hold many events such as cookie bake sales, garage sales, and beach or park recycling days to raise money.As we started doing events together, I noticed that the club was not very integrated, or close-knit. I struggled to get to know the male-dominated group, often unsure of how to breach the invisible barrier that seemed to keep us from connecting. I began by attempting to broach conversations with them more frequently and trying to find things in common. Slowly, they started opening up to me; we began meeting up to coordinate events together. Last year, we decided to bake cookies to sell. To break the tension, instead of letting each person do different things, I placed all the club members ingroups so they would need to interact during each step. It became a team building exercise of my own creation, that ultimately brought us closer together, something I had not anticipated. Whenever I mention our baking event they all laugh and agree in unison about how fun and memorable that day was. At first, I thought being the president of this club would prove simple - I got to make decisions, plan events and take part in community service opportunities.
